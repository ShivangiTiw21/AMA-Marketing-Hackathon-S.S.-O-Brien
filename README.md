# S.S. O'Brien Liberty Ship — Multi-Channel Marketing Strategy

Runner-up project at the American Marketing Association (AMA) Marketing Hackathon in San Francisco. Developed a comprehensive digital marketing strategy for a historic WWII Liberty Ship museum operating on a limited budget.

## The Challenge

The S.S. Jeremiah O'Brien is a national historic landmark — a WWII Liberty Ship museum in San Francisco. The challenge: design a high-impact marketing strategy to increase visitor traffic and ticket sales with minimal budget, in just 24 hours.

## My Role

Key strategist on the team. I led social media strategy and community partnerships, focusing on high-impact, low-cost tactics that the museum could realistically implement.

## The Strategy

- Structured a $10,000/month Google Ads Grant into four targeted campaigns: General Tourism, Fleet Week Promotions, Educational Programs, and Local Community Engagement
- Designed social media content strategy with branded hashtags and user-generated content campaigns
- Built influencer partnership framework with local history and travel creators
- Created email marketing sequences for different audience segments (tourists, schools, veterans, locals)
- Developed a volunteer ambassador program for community-driven promotion

## Projected Impact

- 100,000+ impressions per month across channels
- 150+ new monthly website visitors from Google Ads alone
- 8-15% anticipated click-through rate on targeted campaigns
- Stronger local community engagement and veteran outreach

## Result

Runner-up at the AMA SF Marketing Hackathon. Judges specifically praised how practical and feasible the strategy was despite resource constraints.

## What I Learned

Constraints force creativity. With no real budget, you get laser-focused on what actually moves the needle. Every tactic in our plan was implementable with free or near-free tools.
